About Me

I got into the software biz in 1991 back before the internet, before the introduction of Microsoft Windows and the mouse.

I worked as a Software Configuration Manager and Unix/QNX admin for Beckman Instruments and went to school at night working towards a degree in Computer Science. I eventually graduated from California State University of Fullerton, CA with my BS in CS and immediatlely went to work for TRW as a developer integrating satelite telemetry feeds into a health and monitoring system. In 1996 I became a member of the development team at Schriever AFB working on the user interface of the war gaming test bed which emulated the systems used in the Cheyene Mountain facility.

In 1997 I hired on with MCI in Colorado Springs and worked in the telecommunications field. My team created a voice recognition platform which used the Nuance speech engine. We created custom speech applications using Java Servlets, VoiceXML/GRXML/CSS, and javascript.

MCI eventually became part of Verizon and after almost 18 years I was involved in a RIFF. Two months later RELX Group hired me as their Interactive Voice Response (IVR) expert where I assumed responsibility for all of their self service IVR applications and integrations working with Java on the Avaya platform. We eventually migrated the applications from the in-house Avaya platform to the inContact cloud based platform. RELX Group then made the decision to move most of the inContact platform support to the Philippines and, after 4 years with the company, I was again involed in a RIFF.

I then made the decision to enroll in a Full Stack Web Development bootcamp with the goal of eventually getting onto a web development team.
